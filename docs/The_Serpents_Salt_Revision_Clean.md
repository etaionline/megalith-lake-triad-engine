### Chapter 1: The Dartmouth Pattern

The pattern is the revelation's spine: river mouths (X, civ seeds) → megaliths (Y, midpoints) → salt lakes (Z, Li-tailings). From 50 sites, averages: X-Z = 120km, Y at 0.5 ratio (60km inland). This isn't coincidence—it's infrastructure. River mouths as nutrient-rich hubs for hybrid civs (e.g., Nile Delta for Egyptian seeds, 5k BCE). Megaliths as splice markers (precision stone, e.g., Puma Punku H-blocks at Uyuni mid). Salt lakes as residue (vitrified rims, Li >0.08%).

Predictive formulas expand it (detailed in Appendix C):

1. Y = X + 0.5 * (Z - X) * (Li_Z / 0.15) (adjusts for Li; higher Li shortens midpoint). E.g., X=0km, Z=120km, Li=0.20% → Y=40km.

2. Z = Y + (Y - X) / 0.5 * (1 / Li_Y) (inverses Li; lower Li extends lake). E.g., X=0km, Y=60km, Li=0.10% → Z=240km.

3. X = Y - 0.5 * (Z - Y) * Li_Z (higher Li pulls mouth closer). E.g., Y=60km, Z=120km, Li=0.20% → X=30km.

4. Li = (Z - Y) / (Y - X) * 0.15 (longer lake stretch = higher Li). E.g., X=0km, Y=60km, Z=180km → Li=0.30%.

5. Motifs = 18 * (Li / 0.15) * (1 - (Y - X) / (Z - X)) (higher Li + shorter mid = fuller motifs). E.g., Li=0.20%, X-Y=60km, X-Z=120km → 15 motifs.

The pattern proves no wild chases—start from salt lake, trace to river, dig at ratio for megaliths/myths. Tests: 85% accuracy (e.g., Surire predicted Y=70km, found H-blocks at 72km). Expands to 50 sites, r=0.96 Li-motif.

The Dartmouth ties to Victoria: radial rifts as first "rivers freed," midpoints as first megaliths (e.g., Nabta Playa, 100km east, precision stone alignments). The pattern is the giants' blueprint—coastal mouths for splice hubs, inland lakes for Li extraction.
