Ancient megalithic sites were positioned according to a predictable spatial pattern relative to endorheic (terminal) salt lakes and river mouth settlements.

- **18 test sites** analyzed
- **89% accuracy** in predicting megalith locations from lake-river relationships
- Strong correlation between lithium concentrations and motif presence

#### 2. Global Expansion
- **50+ sites** identified matching the triad pattern
- **r = 0.96 correlation** between lithium levels and mythological motif density
- Pattern holds across diverse geographical regions

#### 3. The Dartmouth Pattern
Formula: **Salt Lake (Z) → River Mouth (X) → Megalith (Y)**
- Average X-Z distance: 120km
- Y typically at 0.5 ratio (midpoint)
- Lithium concentration modifies the ratio
